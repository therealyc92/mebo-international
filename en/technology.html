MEBT Technology

Moist Exposed Burn Therapy represents a paradigm shift: instead of desiccating the wound, it creates an environment that mimics the natural physiological conditions of healthy skin.

The Principle

Regeneration, not just scarring

Conventional burn treatment is based on keeping the wound dry, which causes tissue desiccation, intense pain, and the formation of eschars that prevent cell migration.

Moist Exposed Burn Therapy (MEBT), developed by Professor Xu Rongxiang, maintains the wound bed in a physiological moist environment that allows keratinocytes to migrate, proliferate, and restore the epidermis naturally.

The result: less pain, fewer infections, faster regeneration, and significantly reduced scarring.

Mechanism of Action

The four pillars of MEBT

MEBT technology operates through four complementary mechanisms that transform the healing process.

1. Physiological moist environment

MEBO Ointment forms a semi-permeable protective layer over the wound that retains natural moisture, prevents tissue desiccation, and maintains a pH favorable to cellular activity. This eliminates the desiccation damage that keratinocytes suffer in dry treatments.

2. Cellular regeneration

The moist environment stimulates migration and proliferation of keratinocytes from the wound edges and skin appendages, accelerating re-epithelialization. The natural architecture of the skin is restored rather than the formation of scar tissue.

3. Triple action: analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial

The active components of MEBO Ointment exert a synergistic effect: they relieve pain by protecting exposed nerve endings, reduce tissue inflammation, and possess broad-spectrum antibacterial activity, reducing the risk of infection without aggressive topical antibiotics.

4. Reduced scarring

By favoring physiological regeneration over scar repair, MEBT significantly reduces the formation of fibrous tissue. The regenerated skin shows better texture, color, and elasticity, substantially improving aesthetic and functional outcomes.

Composition

Active ingredients of MEBO Ointment

A unique formulation that combines active principles from traditional medicine with a modern pharmaceutical base.

β-Sitosterol

Phytosterols with anti-inflammatory properties that reduce tissue inflammation and favor epithelial regeneration.

Baicalein

Flavonoid compound with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activity that protects tissue from oxidative damage.

Berberine

Alkaloid with proven broad-spectrum antimicrobial activity that prevents infections in the wound bed.

Pharmaceutical base: The active ingredients are formulated in a base of beeswax and sesame oil, which provides the ideal texture to maintain the moist environment and facilitate application and removal without damaging regenerated tissue.
