Indications

What is MEBO Ointment used for?

Burns

Partial-thickness burns (superficial and deep), thermal and scald burns. Promotes epidermal regeneration with minimal pain and scarring.

Chronic Ulcers

Pressure ulcers (bedsores), venous ulcers, and arterial ulcers. Facilitates debridement and tissue granulation.

Diabetic Foot

Lesions and ulcers of the foot in patients with diabetes. Helps prevent amputation through tissue regeneration.

Donor Sites

Skin graft harvesting sites. Accelerates donor area healing and reduces postoperative pain.

Application

Mode of use

General MEBO Ointment application protocol. Specific application must be indicated by a healthcare professional.

1

Clean

Gently clean the affected area with saline solution. Do not use aggressive antiseptics.

2

Apply

Apply a uniform layer of MEBO Ointment 1–2 mm thick over the entire wound surface.

3

Cover

Cover with sterile non-adherent dressing as indicated. Renew every 4–6 hours or as prescribed.

4

Reapply

Clean residual ointment from previous application and reapply. Continue until complete re-epithelialization.

Application frequency and treatment duration depend on the type, size, and severity of the lesion. Always follow the instructions of your treating healthcare professional.

Methods

Dressing change

The application method varies depending on the type of lesion and clinical environment. Consult your healthcare professional to determine the most appropriate method.

Exposed method

Every 4-6 hours

Apply MEBO

Spread a 1 mm thick layer of MEBO on the wound.

Semi-exposed method

Every 8-12 hours

Apply MEBO

Spread a 1-2 mm thick layer of MEBO on the wound.

Cover

Cover with 1-2 layers of gauze impregnated with MEBO.

Dressing method

Every 12-24 hours

Apply MEBO

Spread a 2-3 mm thick layer of MEBO on the wound.

Cover

Cover with 2-4 layers of gauze impregnated with MEBO.

Secure

Cover with a cotton pad 1-3 cm thick. Secure with a bandage applying light pressure.

Safety

Safety information

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to any of the components
  • Allergy to bee products (beeswax)
  • Full-thickness burns requiring surgical escharotomy

Precautions

  • External use only. Do not ingest
  • Avoid contact with eyes
  • During pregnancy or lactation, consult your doctor
  • Do not use on penetrating wounds without medical supervision

Adverse Reactions

  • Rarely: transient local irritation
  • Very rarely: cutaneous allergic reaction
  • In case of adverse reaction, discontinue use and consult your doctor

Important notice: This information is for guidance only and does not replace professional medical advice. Always consult your doctor or pharmacist before using this product. Keep out of the reach of children.
