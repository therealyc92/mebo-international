Pharmacological Data

Detailed technical information

Pharmacodynamics

MEBO Ointment exerts its therapeutic effect through multiple complementary mechanisms:

  • Barrier effect: Forms a semi-permeable film that protects the wound while maintaining physiological moisture
  • Anti-inflammatory activity: Inhibition of pro-inflammatory mediators (PGE2, TNF-α, IL-6)
  • Antimicrobial activity: Broad-spectrum bacteriostatic and bactericidal action without developing resistance
  • Regenerative stimulation: Promotes release of endogenous growth factors (EGF, FGF)

Pharmacokinetics

As a topical product, systemic absorption is minimal:

  • Active components act locally on the wound bed
  • Systemic absorption is negligible on intact skin and low on damaged skin
  • No significant plasma concentrations detected in toxicokinetic studies
  • Elimination mainly through removal of residual ointment during dressing changes

Preclinical safety

  • Acute toxicity: LD50 > 5,000 mg/kg (oral route in rodents) — classified as practically non-toxic
  • Cutaneous irritation: Non-irritant in Draize model
  • Sensitization: Non-sensitizer in Magnusson-Kligman test
  • Mutagenicity: Negative in Ames test and micronucleus assay

Drug interactions

  • No significant drug interactions have been described
  • Not recommended to combine with aggressive topical antiseptics (iodine, peroxide) that may alter the moist environment
  • Can be used complementarily with systemic antibiotic therapy when indicated
  • Consult the complete technical sheet for detailed information
