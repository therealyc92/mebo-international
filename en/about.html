The Pioneer

Father of regenerative life science

Professor Xu Rongxiang was a Chinese physician and researcher who revolutionized burn treatment by developing Moist Exposed Burn Therapy (MEBT) in the 1980s.

His vision challenged the traditional paradigm of keeping wounds dry, demonstrating that a physiological moist environment favors natural cellular regeneration and significantly reduces pain, infection and scarring.

He founded the Guangming Burn Wound and Ulcer Research Institute in Beijing and dedicated his life to the research of human organ regeneration, laying the foundations of what is now known as Regenerative Life Science.

Historic milestone: In 1988, MEBO Ointment received the first new drug certificate for traditional Chinese medicine issued by China's Ministry of Health for a burn ointment.

Our Journey

MEBO International milestones

Three decades of continuous innovation, global expansion, and commitment to regenerative science.

1987

Foundation

Professor Xu Rongxiang establishes the Guangming Burn and Ulcer Research Institute in Beijing, laying the foundations of MEBO.

1988

First Certificate

MEBO Ointment receives the first new drug certificate for traditional Chinese medicine from China's Ministry of Health for burn treatment.

1991

National Expansion

MEBT technology is adopted in more than 4,000 hospitals in China, transforming the standard of burn treatment.

1995

U.S. Patent

MEBO obtains the U.S. patent for its burn ointment formula, marking the beginning of its internationalization.

2000s

Global Presence

MEBO expands to more than 70 countries in Asia, the Middle East, Africa, Europe and Latin America, treating millions of patients.

2019

FDA Clearance

MEBO Ointment receives FDA 510(k) clearance in the United States as a medical device for the treatment of burns.

Today

Continuing Leadership

With presence in 73+ countries and more than 45 scientific publications, MEBO continues to lead innovation in regenerative medicine.
